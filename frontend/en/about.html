CEO Message

The year 2025 marked a qualitative turning point in the journey of the Real Estate Development Fund, focusing on crystallizing the Fund's renewed role in the coming phase, based on the goals of Vision 2030 and the Housing Program. This represents an extension of the Fund's long history in meeting and supporting the needs of various stages of our nation's development.
This came within a national context witnessing tangible progress in homeownership, as the homeownership rate in the Kingdom reached 66.2%, on a steady path toward the Saudi Vision 2030 target of 70%, reflecting the maturity and integration of the housing ecosystem.
The Fund's programs enabled the conclusion of 83,014 subsidized housing finance contracts during the year, confirming the continued real demand for ownership and reinforcing the Fund's role as a stability factor and a primary driver in the housing finance system. We also continued to reshape our service model through digital transformation, with the majority of transactions now completed electronically within a highly reliable operational framework, leading to improved resource allocation and efficiency.
Strategic partnerships remained a cornerstone in executing our priorities, with agreements signed with 17 developers to support more than 3,285 housing units, alongside enhancing integration with financial institutions and government agencies across the value chain in the housing sector. Our subsidiaries contributed to supporting system stability and enhancing confidence, strengthening the governance of guarantee portfolios and raising capital management efficiency. Furthermore, agreements exceeding SAR 2 billion were signed to expand housing supply, in partnership with Dhamanat and ICBC, providing new liquidity and restructuring costs, while realigning the branch network based on efficiency considerations by approximately SAR 3.5 billion to support National Housing Company projects.

H.E. Mr. Mansour bin Madi

The National Financing Services Support Company continues to develop its operations by attracting an international partner with extensive global experience to raise the level and efficiency of financing products, collection services, and cost-effectiveness for financiers and beneficiaries.
In light of demographic shifts and changing demand patterns, it has become essential to develop more comprehensive and flexible financing solutions without compromising risk standards. Consequently, the Fund began developing alternative models, including rent-to-own.
Joint ownership and improved guarantee frameworks to reduce down payment barriers enhance financial inclusion and target priority segments within a balanced and responsible financial framework. During the year, we launched our new corporate strategy, based on the clear principle that financial sustainability is a prerequisite for the sustainability of developmental impact. Out of 24 strategic initiatives, 18 were launched, focusing on enhancing asset quality, improving capital management, raising operational efficiency, and developing more flexible intervention tools responsive to market shifts within a clear governance framework and updated risk management policies.
This orientation was reflected in a tangible improvement in the Fund's financial position, as the outstanding loan portfolio decreased by 7.5% to reach SAR 100 billion due to improved effective repayment rates, contributing to lower credit risks and a stronger balance sheet. Collections through the early repayment program increased by approximately SAR 800 million during the year, while revenues from loans, fees, and developer programs rose by 11% to reach SAR 949 million, indicating a sustainable improvement in operational efficiency and cash flow quality. Looking ahead, maintaining the momentum of homeownership is a priority that requires disciplined institutional execution and close partnerships with financial institutions and our partners in the housing ecosystem, ensuring REDF's continued position as a primary driver in the Kingdom's housing finance system to support a new generation of homeownership.
