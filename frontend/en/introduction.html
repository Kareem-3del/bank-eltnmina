Overview of REDF and its Goals

The Real Estate Development Fund was established by Royal Decree No. M/23 on 11/06/1394H (01/07/1974). Its purpose is to lead real estate development in Saudi Arabia by enabling beneficiaries to own suitable housing.

By providing financing and savings solutions, and keeping pace with development challenges and growth requirements, it contributes to achieving the goals of the Housing Program and Saudi Vision 2030.

Enhancing Financial Integration to Empower the Future of Housing

2025 marked a significant turning point as the Real Estate Development Fund began implementing its updated corporate strategy, designed to empower and drive the growth of the housing market in Saudi Arabia by providing sustainable and innovative financial solutions. This strategy serves citizens and all market stakeholders, enhancing the efficiency of the financing ecosystem and supporting homeownership targets.

By year-end, the citizen homeownership rate reached 66.2%, steadily progressing towards the Saudi Vision 2030 goal of 70%. This achievement reflects not only increased housing opportunities but also the continuous development of a system designed for wide-scale impact.

The system is capable of responding to economic cycles and demographic shifts to meet the aspirations of the housing sector and the youth. While owning a home remains a deep-rooted ambition—given the Kingdom's youthful society and generational shifts—joining the housing market for the first home is now seen as more than a necessity; it is a long-term investment and a tool for financial and social stability.

The impact of homeownership extends beyond the family, contributing to financial stability, reducing exposure to rental market fluctuations, and strengthening individual financial positions. Nationally, the housing sector supports GDP, ensures job sustainability across various sectors, and enhances social cohesion.

From this perspective, housing is a social priority and a key economic policy tool for building a solid nation. Within this framework, REDF plays a pivotal role in providing sustainable and innovative financing solutions.

Through close cooperation with our partners and market participants, the Fund supports a diverse portfolio of solutions reflecting the varied needs of Saudi citizens, considering their different financial capabilities. This approach yielded tangible results in 2025, where 83,014 beneficiaries benefited from support programs with a total subsidized financing of 52.7 billion SAR. Since 2017, 920,126 beneficiaries have owned their homes through programs provided by the Fund, with total subsidized financing exceeding 613 billion SAR.

Maintaining this progress requires disciplined governance and an effective institutional framework. In 2025, REDF launched a corporate strategy based on four main pillars, placing financial sustainability at its core. Simultaneously, the ecosystem has evolved to be more resilient, digitally adaptive, and ready to execute strategic goals efficiently.

Collaboration across the financial and housing ecosystem remains a cornerstone of achievement. During the year, the Fund participated in major events such as Cityscape and the Momentum conference, signing several MOUs to stimulate housing supply and support private sector participation. These efforts reflect a commitment to developing a housing system that achieves both scale and quality.

In conclusion, I extend my sincere gratitude to our wise leadership, the Custodian of the Two Holy Mosques, King Salman bin Abdulaziz Al Saud, and HRH Prince Mohammed bin Salman Al Saud, Crown Prince and Prime Minister, for their guidance and support. I also thank the Board of Directors, our government and private sector partners, and the employees of the Real Estate Development Fund for their continuous efforts in achieving our goals.
